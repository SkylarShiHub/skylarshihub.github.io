## About Me

I am a first year Ph.D. student in Statistics at the Ohio State University (OSU). I will be working as a Research Assistant with Xiaotian Zheng <a href="https://www.xzheng42.com/">Xiaotian Zheng</a> in Summer 2026, focusing on completing our working paper titled “Mark-specific Quantile Function Learning on Temporal Point Processes.”

Before this, I worked with <a href="https://sites.stat.washington.edu/raftery/">Adrian E. Raftery</a> at University of Washington focused on modeling time series based on Bayesian hierarchical models and Bayesian Model Averaging, applied to global temperature forecast. Additionally, I worked with <a href="https://sites.stat.washington.edu/people/abelrod/Home.html">Abel Rodriguze</a> on a one-dimensional factor analysis model, the Probit Unfolding Model, aimed at estimating individual ideology over time from binary preference data.   


%## Research Interests

%- **Bayesian statistics:**  hierarchical models, Bayesian Model Averaging
%- **Latent variable models:**  factor analysis models
